"the origin of la leyenda negra"

In 1508, a Spaniard named Bartolome de las Casas joined a voyage bound for the Caribbean (Hispanola). During this trip, Las Casas saw first hand what colonization was like and decided soon to become a Friar instead of supporting it.
The Dominicans Friars on Hispaniola decided very quickly as a group that the indigenous peoples of the Americas deserved better treatment. In 1511 Las Casas wrote A Brief Account of the Destruction of the Indies. In this account he detailed the colonization he saw.
Las Casas' writings painted the Spaniards as vicious wolves and the natives as helpless sheep. His writings depicted the Conquistadors as savage and animalistic.
Later, these writings were used as propaganda against the Spanish by Protestant countries like England and France. They frame the Spanish as evil and cruel, despite the fact that thse countries were also participating in colonization. This provided anti-hispanic rhetoic used by competing countries in colonization.
